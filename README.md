The Formula, Explained Simply

Every concrete estimate — slabs, footings, columns, steps — starts with the same three-number multiplication:

Volume (cubic feet) = Length × Width × Thickness

Then divide by 27 to get cubic yards:

Cubic Yards = Volume (ft³) ÷ 27

That number 27 comes from the fact that one cubic yard is a 3 ft × 3 ft × 3 ft cube — 27 cubic feet. Ready-mix concrete in the U.S. is ordered, priced, and delivered by the cubic yard. That's why the conversion matters.

The one step most people mess up? Thickness. Slabs are almost always described in inches on a job site, but the formula needs feet. A 4-inch slab thickness is not "4" — it's 0.333 (divide 4 by 12). Enter "4" instead and your estimate is 12 times too large. The calculator handles this automatically, but if you're ever working it out by hand, convert inches to feet first.

For circular shapes — round patios, columns, Sonotube footings — the formula changes:

Volume (ft³) = π × Radius² × Depth
            = 3.14159 × (Diameter ÷ 2)² × Depth

Then divide by 27 for cubic yards, same as always.


How to Use the Calculator Step by Step

Head to the CalculationHub.org concrete calculator. Here's what each step actually means:

Select your shape. Most projects are rectangular (slabs, footings, walls). Circular handles round patios and cylindrical piers. Sonotube/tube form is purpose-built for deck footings.

Enter dimensions. Use feet for length and width. For thickness, use inches — the calculator converts for you. Measure the inside of your forms. The outside dimension includes the form boards themselves, which don't fill with concrete.

Set your quantity. Pouring multiple identical footings? Enter the count and let the calculator multiply. Ten identical tube footings calculated as one and then multiplied is faster and less error-prone than calculating ten times.

Check the outputs. You'll see cubic yards (what to tell a ready-mix supplier), cubic feet (useful for bag calculations), and bag counts for common sizes. Use all three — they cross-check each other.
Apply waste. Add 5–10% to whatever the calculator shows before placing your order. The calculator will suggest this; take the recommendation seriously.
